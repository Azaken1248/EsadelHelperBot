## TL;DR

- Akiyama Mizuki is the video/MV editor of the underground circle **25-ji, Nightcord de.** ("Niigo"/Nightcord at 25:00), a fashion-obsessed, cheerful mediator whose central arc is a years-long fear of losing their found family if a hidden "secret" — heavily coded as being transgender/gender-nonconforming — comes to light; Mizuki is the only character in the game whose official gender is listed as "?".
- That arc paid off in the October 2024 JP event **"Whither This Path of Thorns"** (Mizu5), where Mizuki is involuntarily outed to Ena, and its December 2024 sequel **"With Our Wounded Hands,"** where Ena finds Mizuki, affirms the friendship, and Mizuki tells Niigo the secret in their own words — with the EN server running these one year behind (Oct/Dec 2025).
- Mizuki is voiced by **Hinata Satō**, uses the pronoun **"boku"** in Japanese, was designed by Colorful Palette's illustration team (supervised by iXima) rather than one credited artist, and remains one of the game's most popular and most fandom-debated characters, especially around pronoun usage.

## Key Findings

- **Identity and framing.** Mizuki (暁山瑞希) is a second-year at Kamiyama High School and Niigo's animator, going by the online alias **"Amia."** Their profile gender is officially "Unknown/?" — unique in the cast. Per Sekaipedia: "While the gender of all characters in Project SEKAI is listed as 'Male' or 'Female' on their character profiles, Mizuki's is listed as 'Unknown'." (The official site pjsekai.sega.jp lists 性別/gender as "?".) In Japanese, the script avoids gendered words for Mizuki; in the English localization, "Though the Global translation used she/her a couple of times early on in the game's lifecycle, pronouns are presently avoided for Mizuki in the translation, with they/them being used when absolutely necessary."
- **The secret.** Across multiple focus events, the "secret" Mizuki hides from Niigo is heavily coded as gender identity. The Mizu5 event confirmed Mizuki's assigned sex is male while deliberately leaving the gender label unstated, sustaining the "?" designation. The core fear is not rejection but that friends' well-meaning "kindness" would change how they are treated, breaking the "utopia" of being accepted as an ordinary friend.
- **Emotional arc.** Mizuki begins as the bright, teasing "glue" of a deeply troubled group, presenting a carefree self while concealing bullying trauma and, like every Niigo member, a wish to "disappear." The arc moves through avoidance, an involuntary outing, near-total withdrawal, and finally acceptance via Ena, ending in a more stable Mizuki able to exist openly among friends.
- **Relationships.** Mizuki's most important bond is with Shinonome Ena (banter partner, eventual confidante); they parallel Mafuyu (both hide their "true selves"), are grateful to Kanade (whose music "kept them going"), and have deep ties to Kamishiro Rui (middle-school rooftop friend and fellow outcast) and older sister Yuuki (fashion designer abroad who encouraged Mizuki's self-expression).
- **Production.** Voiced by Hinata Satō (former Sakura Gakuin idol; also Junna in Revue Starlight, Leah in Love Live! Sunshine!!). Designed by the Colorful Palette illustration team with iXima supervising. Mizuki's commissioned focus songs include IDSMILE (toa), kitty (Tsumiki), and Bake no Hana (Nakiso).

## Profile and Trivia

- **Full name:** Akiyama Mizuki (暁山瑞希). Surname Akiyama: 暁 (aki/akatsuki, "dawn," specifically the darkest hours before sunrise) + 山 (yama, "mountain"); like all Niigo members, the surname encodes a time of day (Kanade = evening 宵, Mafuyu = morning 朝, Ena = daybreak 東雲, Mizuki = dawn 暁). Given name Mizuki (瑞希): 瑞 ("auspicious sign") + 希 ("hope"); a unisex name. The 希 (ki) is shared with sister Yuuki's name.
- **Alias:** "Amia," derived from Mia, the villain of a magical-girl anime Mizuki loved (rendered as "Miracle Magical Girl☆Lala" in some wiki summaries).
- **Birthday:** August 27 (Virgo). **Height:** ~163–165 cm (listed as 163cm→165cm on some databases, reflecting a model update). **School:** Kamiyama High School; Year 1 in Class 1-A (with Shiraishi An), Year 2 in Class 2-B (with Aoyagi Toya). Mizuki skips class frequently, attending only enough (supplementary classes) to avoid repeating a grade.
- **Family:** Older sister Akiyama Yuuki, a fashion designer living overseas, who made cute clothes for Mizuki and encouraged them to follow their heart despite criticism.
- **Likes/hobbies:** anything cute, fashion, sewing/redesigning clothes, making collages and MVs, anime and idols (a big fan of MORE MORE JUMP!'s Hanasato Minori — chants "L-O-V-E Mino-ri!!"). Food: likes spicy flavors but strongly dislikes food that is too hot in temperature.
- **First-person pronoun:** "boku" (ボク), written in katakana — a masculine pronoun typically used by young boys but also by tomboyish girls, giving an exaggeratedly cute, distinctive quality. Others address Mizuki as "Amia" (Niigo online), "Mizuki," "Mizuki-chan," "Mizuki-san," or "Akiyama" depending on the character; Mizuki calls Akito "otouto-kun" (li'l bro).
- **Appearance:** light pink hair in a side ponytail with two loose face-framing strands, light pink eyes with a single pink eyelash, red heart-ribbon hair tie; a candy is often shown in official illustrations.

## Personality and Mannerisms

Mizuki is bright, energetic, teasing, and free-spirited — the most outwardly cheerful Niigo member and its mediator, defusing Ena–Mafuyu arguments with a quip or deflection. They are highly emotionally intelligent, quick to notice others' stress and to set boundaries, and are described by Ena as secretly the most mature member. Beneath this is a conflict-avoidant, emotionally sensitive person afraid of pain who keeps their own feelings hidden. Fan personality typings commonly peg Mizuki as ESFJ and Enneagram 2w3 ("the Helper"), with a basic fear of being unwanted/unloved — consistent with the canon. The bright, clothes-making persona is presented as the true self; a colder, defensive front appears toward those who mock them, a learned defense mechanism from years of bullying. Mizuki's signature tagline is **"I welcome anything as long as it's cute♪,"** and their "Brand New World" bio quote is **"I'm still me, but is that ok?"**

## Backstory

Mizuki loved cute clothing from childhood; wearing outfits their sister made drew both compliments and judgment, and after being bullied Mizuki asked Yuuki whether to give up cute things — Yuuki told them to follow their heart while warning the criticism would keep hurting. Bullied since elementary school for their fashion and honesty, Mizuki began skipping school. In middle school, on the rooftop, Mizuki met Kamishiro Rui (now of Wonderlands×Showtime); the two outcasts became close and, per the game's framing, "kept each other alive." After Rui graduated, Mizuki became more isolated. Mizuki later found one of Kanade's songs by chance, made an MV using Shinonome Ena's art (with permission), and Kanade invited both to join Niigo — Mizuki as video editor, Ena as illustrator.

## 25-ji, Nightcord de. and the Empty SEKAI

Niigo is an anonymous underground circle founded by composer Yoisaki Kanade ("K") to make music that "saves people." Members: Kanade (compose), Asahina Mafuyu/"Yuki" (lyrics/mix), Ena/"Enanan" (illustration), Mizuki/"Amia" (MV). They communicate via the "Nightcord" chat app; the name references Discord. Internally, the developers' files reportedly label the unit "school refusal," reflecting each member's fraught relationship with school (Kanade does online classes, Mafuyu faces intense academic pressure, Ena takes night classes, Mizuki skips class). Their SEKAI is the Empty SEKAI, a barren void born solely from Mafuyu's feelings, where Miku (and later Rin, Len, Luka, MEIKO, KAITO) reside. Every member has, at some point, wished to "disappear," and the unit is the game's heaviest, exploring depression, family pressure, self-worth, and Mizuki's bullying/identity struggle. MEIKO is notably positioned as the VIRTUAL SINGER who understands Mizuki best.

## The Mizuki-Focus Event Arc (chronological, JP)

1. **Secret Distance (Apr 2021)** — first Niigo focus event; Mizuki organizes a "mystery tour" to inspire a slumping Kanade, making destinations haunted. Mizuki cannot commit to "next year," fearing they'd have to reveal the secret to stay. Focus song: IDSMILE (toa), whose lyrics directly encode the theme (identity you won't yield; a secret you can't part with).
2. **What lies behind. What lies ahead. (2021)** — Ena notices Mizuki's distance and vows to wait "forever" for Mizuki to open up.
3. **We Escape to Survive** — focus song kitty (Tsumiki); themes of loneliness and running.
4. **Next to the Unchanging Warmth (Dec 2023–Jan 2024)** — Yuuki returns for New Year; childhood flashbacks show Mizuki's early conflict over cute clothing; sister's encouragement reframed.
5. **Reeling in the Lights / Reaching (2024)** — Mafuyu confronts her father; inspired, Mizuki resolves to tell Niigo the secret.
6. **Whither This Path of Thorns (Mizu5, Oct 12–19 2024 JP; Oct 2025 EN)** — Mizuki plans to tell Ena first at the school festival but is delayed helping a classmate; classmates out Mizuki to Ena by misgendering ("is Akiyama a guy too?"). Mizuki flees, cuts contact, retreats into the Empty SEKAI. Focus song: Bake no Hana (Nakiso). Post-story mechanic: after Episode 8, Mizuki temporarily disappears from the Real World home screen, appearing alone in the Empty SEKAI lake with lightless eyes and a monotone voice.
7. **With Our Wounded Hands (Dec 2024 JP; Dec 2025 EN)** — Ena searches for Mizuki; MEIKO helps corner the situation; Ena rejects Mizuki's self-narrative of inevitable abandonment and affirms the friendship ("…Let's stay together, Mizuki."). Mizuki breaks down, accepts Ena, rejoins Niigo, and tells the group and VIRTUAL SINGERS the secret in their own words. Afterward Mizuki is shown stable and back to their usual self.
8. **Reaching Out to a Tomorrow That Won't Come Unraveled (later)** — via Yuuki, Mizuki forms a dream of becoming a source of strength for others with similar wounds, affirming they can live as themselves.

## Gender Identity: Canon, Official Handling, and Fandom

Mizuki is the only Project Sekai character with gender "?" — established since pre-launch (VA Satō Hinata tweeted in Dec 2019 that Mizuki's gender was "still a mystery"). The narrative uses dense trans-coding: color symbolism matching the transgender pride flag (notably the Fragment/Empty SEKAI palette and the 6th-focus card "Drawing The Tomorrow As I Am," whose colors are the exact shades of the transgender pride flag), Villain cover choreography, middle-school depictions where Mizuki were male-presenting (male school uniform, shorter hair), and a bedroom-mirror motif (the once-covered mirror later uncovered as they accept their identity). Mizu5 confirmed assigned sex as male while withholding a gender label, which many read as transfeminine/gender-nonconforming. Official English materials avoid pronouns; JP uses "boku" and no gendered descriptors. Chinese (Moegirl) and Korean (Namu) wikis lean toward "male," while English-language wikis use they/them and avoid picking a gender. Fandom debate is intense: the Mizu5 EN release fueled discourse and, in 2025, a "#sheherformizuki" movement pushing the EN server to adopt she/her. Best framing: a deliberately unresolved, structurally ambiguous character whose story vectors toward femininity/acceptance without a fixed label.

## Relationships

- **Shinonome Ena** — closest bond; the pair's banter fills most of Niigo's chat. Mizuki teases Ena as a "tsundere"; Ena is the one who waits for, finds, and affirms Mizuki. The relationship is widely shipped ("Mizuena") and is the emotional core of the resolution.
- **Asahina Mafuyu** — a mirror/foil: both conceal their "true selves." Mafuyu thinks Mizuki over-obsessed with cuteness; Mizuki envies Mafuyu's willingness to keep searching for herself, and advises Mafuyu that running away can be a valid option — advice Mafuyu later acts on.
- **Yoisaki Kanade** — grateful; Kanade's songs "kept Mizuki going," and Kanade accepts Mizuki as they are.
- **Shinonome Akito** — Ena's brother and Mizuki's schoolmate; Mizuki teases him as "otouto-kun."
- **Kamishiro Rui** — middle-school rooftop friend, fellow outcast; Mizuki made Rui and Nene's stage outfits after finding NeneRobo "totally uncute." Rui knows Mizuki's secret.
- **Shiraishi An** — Year-1 classmate and close friend who is aware of Mizuki's struggles and shields them.
- **Aoyagi Toya** — Year-2 classmate. **Akiyama Yuuki** — sister and formative influence. Other friends via events: Minori, Airi, Saki, Shizuku, Shiho, Honami.

## Production and Songs

- **Voice actor:** Hinata Satō (佐藤日向, born 23 December 1998, Yamagata Prefecture; affiliated with Amuse), a founding member of idol unit Sakura Gakuin; best known for voicing Junna Hoshimi in Revue Starlight, Leah Kazuno in Love Live! Sunshine!!, Noa Fukushima in D4DJ, and Mizuki Akiyama. Around Mizu5 she posted that recording was so painful she cried afterward.
- **Character design:** the Colorful Palette illustration team designed all 20 original characters; iXima (Crypton's official Miku illustrator) supervised default designs in 2019. No single artist is officially credited specifically for Mizuki.
- **Commissioned focus songs:** IDSMILE (toa), kitty (Tsumiki), Bake no Hana (Nakiso). Ahead of Mizu5, Nakiso publicly warned (via the official Project Sekai X account, Sept 2024) "there are some realities that can't be helped; please prepare yourselves." Bake no Hana is the shortest commissioned song (under two minutes); its MASTER note count (1003) is read as symbolic of Mizuki (1) separated from the group (3). Mizuki also has SEKAI/Another Vocal versions of Niigo songs.
- **Film:** *Colorful Stage! The Movie: A Miku Who Can't Sing* — the first feature film based on Hatsune Miku. JP theatrical debut January 17, 2025; GKIDS released it in North American cinemas April 17, 2025. Produced by P.A. Works, directed by Hiroyuki Hata, written by Yoko Yonaiyama, character designs by Yuki Akiyama, music by Satoshi Hōno, distributed in Japan by Shochiku; Niigo (and Mizuki) appear.

## JP vs EN Timing

Story content is identical but staggered ~1 year: Mizu5 ran Oct 2024 (JP) / Oct 2025 (EN); With Our Wounded Hands Dec 2024 (JP) / Dec 2025 (EN). The EN localization's evolving pronoun policy (early she/her → name-preferred/they-only) is a key server difference.

## Caveats

- Mizuki's gender label remains officially "?"; anything beyond "assigned sex male, gender deliberately unstated/trans-coded" is interpretation, not confirmed canon.
- No single real-world illustrator is officially credited for Mizuki; the "team + iXima supervision" account is from Japanese Wikipedia's design-process section. (The film's character designer Yuki Akiyama shares a surname with the character purely by coincidence and is unrelated to the game's original design.)
- No direct, named developer quote confirms the "?" gender was an intentional narrative choice; the intentional ambiguity is strongly implied and widely documented but not verified via an on-record producer quote.
- Some numeric trivia (height 163 vs 165) varies by database and model version.
- EN story summaries rely on JP content already released; minor localization wording differences may exist.
